Why I decided to pursue Software Engineering

Growing up, I was always skilled at deductive reasoning. With that said, I excelled in mathematics, and I eventually pursued a Mathematics degree in college. Computer Science was an option, but I decided to go in on what I felt was the most natural to me: theoretical mathematics.

As I was going through college, I had issues developing relationships with my professors and colleagues which seemed to be important to grow in my career. It was a struggle for me to ask for letters of recommendation to apply to summer research programs and for scholarships. After deep introspection, this led me to pursue a path in self-employment and sales that eventually helped me to improve my competency in communication and relationship building.

Flash forward to now. I had seen friends enrolling in coding bootcamps and the income and lifestyle changes they had. It looked appealing because of the natural talent I had for the related skills, and it seemed like much less effort to make money compared to what I was doing in an area I was weaker at. I feel it is the right time to work with my strengths in logic, analysis, and problem solving to pursue a career I can grow further in, as well as incorporate the skills I've picked up while being self-employed. I decided to enroll in Coding Dojo's full time web development course to expedite my transition.

What makes me the right candidate?

I can see things from multiple perspectives, as I am very analytical and constantly thinking about the many dimensions of an issue. I see things both on a big picture level as well as the micro-details. Generally, I am pragmatic, so I will find an appropriate solution given the conditions, but at the same time I like to be comprehensive in my learning so that I can understand all the details as well as identify areas for improvement.
